Fact 2: Lightning strikes

Lightning strikes the Earth about 100 times every single second. This is roughly 8 million lightning strikes per day!

How hot is lightning?

A single bolt of lightning is about 30,000 Kelvin -five times hotter than the surface of the Sun.

Can lightning strike twice?

Yes! Lightning can absolutely and does strike the same place more than once, especially tall structures like towers and trees.

Fact 3: The Deep Ocean

More than 80% pf the world's oceans remain unexplored. Scientists believe there are millions of species living in the deep sea that we have never discovered.

How deep does the ocean go?

The deepest point in the ocean is the Mariana Trench, which reaches the depth of about 11 kilometers deep enough to fit Mount Everest inside with more to spare!

Fun Comparison

If you placed Mount Everest at the bottom of the Mariana Trench, its peak would still be more than 2 kilometers underwater.
